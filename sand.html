Great Sand Dunes National Park - Colorado

How the dunes are formed:

Eolian (wind) processes bring sand from the eroding mountains to the current dunes.

Fluvial processes move the sand back westward during spring snowmelt from the Sangre de Cristo Mountain range.

The subsurface water keeps moisture in the sand, the surface water from the streams only travels 2.5 miles from the base of the mountains before disappearing below the surface.

Ancient Lake Alamosa likely provided some of the sand when it drained to join the Rio Grande to the southeast.
